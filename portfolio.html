Skyline: Rome

Rome was not built in a day; though, it was imagined in just a few short hours. This masterpiece is a part of the "Skyline" theme. Inspired by the popular galaxy spray paint art in Time Square. The intergration of simple shapes and blending is what makes this very detailed design a success. This piece presents recognizable concepts such as glowing neon The Colosseum (Coliseum) to a familar ringed planet (Saturn). The use of different color schemes and contrast shows a distinct different between the foreground and the background. This digital piece transforms a realistic image into something more science fiction.

Created in Photoshop

Blue Moon

Art comes to life only once in a blue moon. The secret to this 3-D effect is in the angle of the picture being taken, the hatching in the shadows, and the various shades of blue that were colored in. Always was amazed by artist from social media posting art that appear to pop out of the paper. This concept turns metaphorical phrases into literal imagery.

Created with Prisma Color Pencils, Graphite Pencil, Gray Paper, and a x-acto knife

Every detail is important...

El Señor Ortiz

He was a wonderful father and grandfather. This portrait was stippled one long night in June. Wanted to create and give my yearly artwork to my GodMother, and found a picture of her father on the wall. Every pixel of the picture caught and kept my attention, from his posture to his facial features. Replicated him was a must. Sketched a rough outline of his face and features first, then stippled the image until it looked just right. R.I.P. Señor Ortiz.

Created with Graphite, Black Pen, and Matboard

Lady Liberty Lips

Giving some light to the Afro American Community. Everyone knows the traditional red, white, and blue American colors (to the right) very well. But not to many people are familiar with the red, black, and green Afro American colors (on the left). Inspired by Marcus Garvey's Flag, the lips on the left represents Afro America. The lips on the right represent Mainstream America.

Created with Prisma Color Pencils and Black Paper
